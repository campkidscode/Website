About the Class

All students in grades 6-8 are welcome to attend this programming class produced in partnership with the Fanwood Library Summer Reading Program.

The class will take place on Tuesdays and Thursdays from 1:30 to 3:00, starting on Tuesday June 28th and ending on Thursday July 21st.

Students will design apps and programs through hands-on activities and lessons guided by mentors.

Speakers from Rutgers Society of Women Engineers as well as teachers from local high schools will show students how they can apply the skills they are learning. The class culminates in a showcase where students will be able to present their individual projects to family and friends.

No experience is necessary to participate!

Schedule

Intro to CS
Day 1

We will be introducing basic programming concepts and logic using block programming.

CS in Art
Day 2

A speaker will discuss the applications of CS in art, followed by 3D modelling!

Intro to CS (2)
Day 3

Students continue developing programming skills and learn to use Python.

Intro to App Design
Day 4

Students learn the principles of design and create an app using MIT Appinventor.

Intro to Robotics
Day 5

A robotics teacher from a local high school visits to conduct a fun activity with robots!

CS in Business
Day 6

A speaker visits to discuss CS in business and students begin work on individual projects.

Individual Projects
Day 7

Students continue to work on their individual projects.

Final Showcase
Day 8

Students present their individual projects to friends and family and attend a speaker panel.
