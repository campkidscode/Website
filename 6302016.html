Week 1 by Nithya Narayanan

  1. Home
  2. Blog Post

Posted on July 3, 2016 at 1:30 PM

This week was the first week of Camp KidsCode at the Fanwood Memorial Library, NJ. Eighteen students, ranging from 5th to 9th grade, started learning introductory programming concepts. Most of the students had no prior experience coding and Tuesday was the first time they had every programmed!

On Tuesday, teachers Brian Maher, Zach Briet, and Nithya Narayanan guided them through their very first program. We showed them how to make the computer print “Hello, World!” The students quickly progressed to more difficult programming concepts, such as variables and conditional statements.They had a great time programming a turtle in trinket.io to move across the screen and draw various shapes in colors.

On Thursday, we introduced the concepts of loops and functions, which are often used to expedite the programming process. Students were able to design more complicated programs using these concepts, programming a turtle to draw multiple triangles of varying sizes and colors.

For those who would like to reference the notes from this week, they can be found here. Next week, an engineering student from Rutgers University will be visiting to conduct a CS in Art class and we will be having an Intro to App Design class next week. Before next class, students are asked to create accounts on Scratch and Tinkercad.
